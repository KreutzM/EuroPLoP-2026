\begin{abstract}
\ac{stem} degree programs provide good job chances also for students with impairments.
\ac{bvi} students are facing difficulties to access graphically represented information like diagrams ore working with graphical simulation tools e.g. for circuit simulation.

Impairments such as \ac{adhd} or dyslexia can significantly limit the ability to read and comprehend long and complex texts with sustained concentration.

Generative artificial intelligence can support students and other people in the academic context to provide accessibility to learning materials.

This paper extends the pattern collection "A Pattern Collection for Generating Accessible Teaching Materials for Blind and Visually Impaired Students in Computer Science and Electrical Engineering" presented at EuroPLoP 2025.

The patterns presented here focus on using AI tools for gaining accessibility to graphically represented content for \ac{bvi} students (\textsc{Accessibility-Aware AI Tutor}). 

The second pattern -- \textsc{Podcast-Like Audio Summaries for Accessible Academic Learning} -- introduces solutions to leverage access to learning and scientific materials for students wit impairments such as \ac{adhd} or dyslexia. 

\keywords{Accessibility  \and Blind and Visually Impaired \and Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) \and  Dyslexia \and Large Language Models.}
\end{abstract}
