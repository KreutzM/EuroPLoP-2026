\section{Conclusion and Future Work}

% KI: D.18 ersetzt die frühere Future-Work-Formulierung, weil das Paper die AI-Erweiterung bereits leistet.
This paper extends the EuroPLoP 2025 pattern collection for accessible teaching materials in \ac{stem} with two AI-mediated patterns. \textsc{Accessibility-Aware AI Tutor} addresses the need for persistent, course-specific, and accessibility-aware support during learning. \textsc{Structured Audio Companion} addresses the need for segmented, source-grounded audio orientation for complex academic materials.

Both patterns show that generative AI is most useful for accessibility when it is embedded in explicit workflows, curated course context, stable accessibility rules, and quality assurance. AI-generated explanations and audio summaries should not replace precise accessible representations of visual \ac{stem} artifacts. Rather, they should help students find, understand, and use such representations.

The patterns also highlight that different learner groups have different needs. \ac{bvi} students require reliable access to the structure and semantics of visual artifacts, while students with \ac{adhd}, dyslexia, or fatigue may particularly benefit from segmentation, auditory reinforcement, and reduced reading load. A single AI output should therefore not be treated as a universal accessibility solution.

Future work will evaluate the two patterns across additional courses and learner groups. We will also refine the relationships within the pattern language, especially where AI-mediated support connects to formal textual representations, generated explanations, sonification, and human-in-the-loop quality assurance.
